# NEWME MEDTECH Global Rules

These rules apply to the entire website, CMS, AI knowledge layer, navigation, content generation, and internal-linking system.

## Positioning

- This is a medical aesthetic technology system website.
- It must not be presented or structured as a product catalog.
- It must not use marketing-heavy, exaggerated, or price-led language.
- Language must remain professional, clear, clinically relevant, and evidence-conscious.

## Required Hierarchy

All information architecture must follow:

```text
SYSTEM → TECHNOLOGY → PRODUCT → APPLICATION
```

- Systems organize clinical and operational needs.
- Technologies explain physical mechanisms.
- Products implement technologies within a system.
- Applications describe the clinical demand supported by the system, technology, and product relationships.

## CMS and Scalability

- The website must remain scalable and fully CMS-driven.
- Published content must be stored as structured CMS entities or CMS-managed page records.
- Route components must be reusable templates rather than manually authored pages.
- Stable IDs and slugs must be used for all entities and relationships.

## Internal-Linking Rules

- All internal linking must follow system logic.
- A system may link to its technologies, products, and applications.
- A technology may link to its parent systems, supported products, and related applications.
- A product may link to its parent system, primary technology, and supported applications.
- An application may link back to the systems, technologies, and products that support it.
- Unrelated products must not be cross-linked merely for promotion.
- Blog articles, FAQs, and case studies may link only to entities supported by their structured CMS relationships.

## Priority

If a component, page, campaign, or content request conflicts with these rules, these global rules take precedence.
